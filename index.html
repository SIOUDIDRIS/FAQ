  • Remove any medical record attachments that may be contributing to the large file size.
  • After removing the attachments, try saving your work again.
By removing the large file attachments, you can reduce the file size and facilitate the saving process in CCMD. However, ensure that the necessary information is still captured and documented appropriately in the patient's record.

What can I do if my DPN is not working?

If your DPN (Diabetic Peripheral Neuropathy) is not working, please try the following troubleshooting steps:
  • Check the battery level of the DPN device and replace if necessary.
  • Ensure that all connections are secure and properly plugged in.
  • Verify the correct placement of the DPN device on the patient's foot or leg.
  • If you are unable to resolve the issue, call Neurometrix Customer Service at 1-888-786-7287 for further assistance.
Proper functioning of the DPN device is essential for accurate assessments and diagnosis of peripheral neuropathy in diabetic patients.

Why does my EOD (End of Day) and chart submission not match up according to Admin, even though I've submitted all my charts?

If your EOD and chart submission do not match up according to Admin, despite having submitted all your charts, please try the following steps:
  • Ensure that you don't have any charts stuck in your CCMD outbox.
  • Connect to a stable internet connection (preferably Wi-Fi) and resubmit any pending charts.
If the issue persists, please reach out to the IT support team for further investigation and assistance. It is important to ensure that all charts are properly submitted and accounted for in the system.

What should I do if my CCMD password isn't working?

If you are experiencing issues with your CCMD password, please try the following steps:
  • Double-check that you are entering the correct password. Passwords are case-sensitive.
  • Ensure that you are using your username (firstname.lastname) and not your work email address to log in.
  • If you continue to have trouble, you can reset your password via the CCMD application. The password reset link will be sent to your Outlook email address.
If you are still unable to log in, please contact the IT team for further assistance. They will be able to help you resolve the password issue and regain access to CCMD.

How do I handle a freezing CCMD app that requires rebooting and re-logging in?

If you encounter a freezing CCMD app that requires rebooting and re-logging in, please follow these steps:
  • Check if there is an updated version of the CCMD application available. Look for the application catalog icon and see if the CCMD app needs updating.
  • If an update is available, install it to ensure that you have the most recent version of the application.
  • If the freezing issue persists, you may need to reboot your device and log in again to CCMD.
By updating the app and performing a device reboot, you can resolve any freezing issues and ensure a smoother experience with CCMD.

Can I reset my CareConnectMD forms after rebooting my iPad? If force quitting the application doesn't work?

Yes, you can reset your CareConnectMD forms after rebooting your iPad. However, please note that resetting the forms may result in the loss of all patient charting you have entered. Proceed with caution.

If force quitting the application does not resolve the issue, you can follow these steps to reset your forms:
  • Go to the settings on your iPad.
  • Find the "Advanced Settings" option.
  • Select the "Reset Forms" option.
By resetting the forms, you can resolve any issues you are experiencing with CareConnectMD. However, be aware that this action will remove any unsaved charting data, so make sure to back up any important information before proceeding.

Should I test both extremities for DPN?

For DPN (Diabetic Peripheral Neuropathy) testing, it is generally sufficient to test only one extremity. Testing both extremities is not necessary unless specifically indicated or if there are specific concerns related to the patient's condition.

How many attempts should I perform if I'm encountering DPN errors?

If you are encountering DPN errors during testing, you can attempt 2-3 tests using the same sensor. If the patient refuses further testing, document the refusal accordingly in the patient's record.

How do I handle charts not being dispatched and needing the IT team to add them to the system? And where do I chart?

If you encounter charts that are not being dispatched and require the IT team to add them to the system, please follow these steps:
  • Chart in the paper template provided by your NP director for the time being.
  • Transfer all the charting information from the paper template to CCMD once the chart is available in the system.
  • Ensure that you do not write sensitive patient identifiers on the paper. Patient initials will suffice for identification.
By following this process, you can continue documenting the necessary information while awaiting the IT team to add the charts to the system. It is important to maintain accurate and complete documentation during this transition period.
